Stroke is a major cause of disability and death worldwide. Early identification of high-risk individuals is crucial for prevention. This project aims to analyse demographic and health factors such as age, hypertension, heart disease, BMI, glucose level, and lifestyle habits to identify patterns associated with stroke occurrence.

# Finding
1. Age:
The analysis shows that stroke occurrence increases significantly with age. Stroke cases are relatively rare among younger individuals but become more common among individuals aged 50 and above.
2. Hypertension:
Individuals with hypertension show a much higher proportion of stroke cases compared to those without hypertension. This indicates that high blood pressure is a major contributing factor to stroke occurrence.
3. Body Mass Index (BMI):
Most stroke cases appear within the BMI range of approximately 20–30, which is also the most common BMI range in the dataset. Extremely high BMI categories show fewer cases due to limited sample sizes rather than lower risk.
4. Glucose Levels:
Higher average glucose levels appear to be associated with increased stroke occurrence, suggesting a relationship between blood sugar levels and stroke risk.
# Recommendations
Based on the findings of this analysis, several recommendations can be made:
1. Encourage regular health screenings, especially for individuals aged 50 and above.
2. Promote blood pressure monitoring and management to reduce hypertension-related stroke risk.
3. Improve heart disease prevention and management programs.
4. Encourage healthy lifestyle habits, including regular physical activity and balanced diets to maintain healthy BMI levels.
5. Strengthen diabetes and blood glucose monitoring programs.
6. Promote smoking cessation initiatives to reduce cardiovascular health risks.
7. Increase public health awareness about stroke risk factors and early warning signs.
# Conclusion
This analysis identified several factors associated with increased stroke occurrence. Age appears to be one of the strongest predictors, with stroke risk increasing substantially among older individuals. Medical conditions such as hypertension and heart disease also show strong associations with stroke.
Lifestyle-related variables such as smoking and high glucose levels may further contribute to stroke risk, emphasizing the importance of preventive healthcare and early medical intervention.
Overall, the findings highlight the importance of monitoring cardiovascular health, maintaining healthy lifestyle habits, and implementing preventive measures to reduce stroke risk.
